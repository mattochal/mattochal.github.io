About Me

I am a 2nd year PhD student (and 3rd year CDT student) at Edinburgh Centre for Robotics - a joint AI research centre between the University of Edinburgh and Heriot-Watt University, UK. I specialize in Few-Shot Learning, Meta-Learning, and Computer Vision algorithms and their real-world application for autonomous robots and systems.

My research is supervised by Dr Sen Wang and Prof Yvan Petillot from Heriot-Watt University, and jointly funded by SeeByte Ltd and EPSRC. I also work closely with Prof Amos Storkey and the BayesWatch research group at University of Edinburgh. My MSc dissertation examined Few-Shot Learning methods on underwater optical and sonar images.

During my undergrad, I also worked with Prof Seb Stein, Dr Enrico Gerding from University of Southampton by merging mechanism design from Game Theory with Reinforcement Learning for fair, online resource allocation in computational clouds. My BSc dissertation focused on cooperation in Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning, supervised by Dr Markus Brede.
